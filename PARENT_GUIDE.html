üéØ Understanding the Learning Levels

üî§ Level 1: Letter Sounds

Ages 5-7 | Pre-K to Grade 1

Skills: Basic phonics, 3-letter CVC words (consonant-vowel-consonant)

Best for: Children just starting to read, or those who struggle with letter sounds

Time: 5-10 minutes per session

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Level 2: Word Families

Ages 6-8 | Grade 1-2

Skills: Rhyming patterns, word chunks, blending

Best for: Children who know basic sounds but struggle with longer words

Time: 10-15 minutes per session

üëÄ Level 3: Sight Words

Ages 6-9 | Grade 1-3

Skills: Recognition of common words, reading fluency

Best for: Building automatic word recognition

Time: 10 minutes per session (repetition is key!)

üìè Level 4: Longer Words

Ages 7-10 | Grade 2-4

Skills: Multi-syllable words, prefixes/suffixes, complex vocabulary

Best for: Children ready for more challenging words

Time: 15-20 minutes per session

üìñ Level 5: Story Time

Ages 7-11 | Grade 2-5

Skills: Reading comprehension, fluency, context clues

Best for: Children ready to read connected text

Time: 15-25 minutes per session

‚úÖ Best Practices for Success

‚úì DO

  • Practice daily (even 5 minutes helps!)
  • Celebrate small wins and progress
  • Let your child choose their level
  • Use headphones in quiet environment
  • Sit together during first few sessions
  • Encourage repetition without pressure
  • Take breaks if child gets frustrated

‚úó DON'T

  • Force long sessions (causes fatigue)
  • Compare to other children
  • Show frustration or impatience
  • Skip levels (build foundation first)
  • Use as punishment or reward
  • Multitask while child is learning
  • Rush through without understanding

üìÖ Sample Learning Schedule

Option 1: Daily Quick Sessions (Recommended)

  • Monday-Friday: 10-15 minutes after school
  • Weekend: 20-minute story time on Saturday
  • Total: 1 hour per week

Option 2: Intensive Sessions

  • Monday, Wednesday, Friday: 20-30 minutes
  • Other days: Optional review
  • Total: 1-1.5 hours per week
üí° Pro Tip: Consistency beats duration! 10 minutes every day is better than 1 hour once a week.

üéì Supporting Different Learning Styles

Visual Learners

  • Focus on the images for each word
  • Have child draw their own pictures of words
  • Use colored sticky notes to mark progress
  • Create a visual word wall at home

Auditory Learners

  • Use the "Hear Word" button multiple times
  • Have child repeat words out loud
  • Practice slow speed for difficult words
  • Record child reading and play it back

Kinesthetic Learners

  • Trace letters in sand or on paper while using app
  • Act out words (jump for "hop", sleep for "rest")
  • Build words with letter magnets
  • Take movement breaks between words

üö® When to Seek Additional Help

Consider professional assessment if your child:
  • Shows no progress after 2-3 months of consistent use
  • Experiences extreme frustration or emotional distress
  • Has difficulty with sounds that most peers have mastered
  • Reverses letters or words frequently (after age 7)
  • Has a family history of dyslexia or learning disabilities

üéÆ Making it Fun

Engagement Ideas:

  1. Word Scavenger Hunt: Find words from the app in real life (on signs, packages, books)

  4. Family Challenge: Siblings or parents learn along

  6. Progress Chart: Visual sticker chart on wall

  • "That was tricky, but you figured it out!"
  • "I'm proud of your effort!"
  • "Let's try it again together."
  • "Everyone learns at their own pace, and you're doing great!"
‚úó Avoid Saying:

  • "Just sound it out!" (without help)
  • "We'll stay here until you get it right."

üîß Technical Tips

For Best Experience:

  • Browser: Chrome, Safari, or Edge (best speech synthesis)
  • Device: Tablet is ideal (larger screen, touch-friendly)
  • Audio: Headphones help focus, but not required
  • Internet: Required for first load, then works offline
  • Screen Time: Blue light filter recommended for evening sessions

Troubleshooting:

  • No sound? Check volume, unmute tab, try different browser
  • Words too fast? Use "Slow Speed" button
  • Progress lost? Don't clear browser data - it erases saved progress
  • Images not loading? Check internet connection, refresh page
